How I Ended Up Living in a Van

March 12, 2025

There wasn't a single dramatic moment when I decided to move into a van. Instead, it happened slowly.

One road trip turned into another. Surfboards stayed in the car longer. Campsites became familiar. The feeling of returning home slowly shifted from a physical place to a collection of routines carried with me.

Mornings began with checking the wind forecast and making coffee near the ocean. Some days felt productive. Others were spent reading, sketching, or watching changing light across the water.

Living on the road taught me that a meaningful life isn't always built through accumulation. Sometimes it grows through subtraction.

With fewer possessions, fewer plans, and fewer expectations, I found more room for attention.

I started noticing details again: the smell of eucalyptus after rain, the shape of driftwood on empty beaches, and the quiet satisfaction of cooking dinner under an open sky.

The road didn't solve every problem. It simply gave me enough space to listen to questions I had been avoiding.

Looking back, living in a van was never really about travel. It was about learning a different relationship with time, place, and myself.
